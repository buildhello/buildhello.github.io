Web Development

At Build Hello, we do web development.

That means we design, build, and style websites.

But that is not the most important part of our web development service.

The most important part of our web development service, is how we do our web development.

Everything about your website, from the technology you choose, to the methods used to build and document your site can be done at varying level of quality.

Your site can be a c+ website, or an a+ website. It can even be a c+ website that is made to easily convert to a+ website, when or if you want to make those changes.

Your site should be built in a way the supports both your immediate needs and your long-term vision, and optimizes your costs along that timeline.

Call us or contact us through through form or e-mail for an assessment and a quote.

We want to do more than put you on the map, we want to put you there in style.

Which came first, the chicken or the egg?

When it comes to web development, there are tangible benefits to starting with a business plan. For example, it's much easier to design a navigation menu when you have an idea of the pages you want your menu to link to and the names you want appearing on the menu.

We recommend writing a plan for your project even if said project is a blog and not a business.

During plan draft, list ideas for logo and brand identity.

If you've yet to draft a business or project plan, feel free to send us a note through our form and we'll provide you with a plan template and links to recommended resources.

With your plan in your hand, it's time to plan your website.

List the functions you want your website to execute.

Will users require memberships?

Will your website facilitate transactions?

Can users contact you through a form?

Will your website have a map?

Are you including a social media campaign in your plan?

5 years out

List the functionality you want from your site today, and the functionality you want five years from today.

How big will your footprint be in five years. Think in terms of numbers of pages.

Do you have a logo and a brand in mind?

What pictures do you want to have on your website? How many?

Go big or go home

Actually, go big or go home isn't always the best approach to web development.

In the case where you have an established business, and you want a new website to commemorate a new era, going big may be in the cards, but in many cases, it makes sense to start with a small website and grow it gradually as your project goes through milestones.

C+ versus A+ websites

An A+ Website will score higher on performance audits, and be from that perspective more attractive to users, but it will distinguish itself in other areas as well.

For example:

Search Engine Ranking

Image management

Script quality (or plugin quality)

Cross-platform performance

Resiliance to obsolescence

Bot Resistance

Lower error (bug) expectancy

Greater ease of use

Better user experience

Higher aesthetic appeal

Better social media performance

Lower development cost

All of the above are well-known reasons why website owners covet A+ websites over C+ websites, except for one item.

The most important difference between an A+ website and a C+ website

The most important difference between an A+ website and a C+ website, is ease in development.

Every platform, framework, and website is like a world unto its own.

When even an experienced developer begins work on a website for the first time, he or she has to study the code base to learn how to implement needed changes.

Developer turn-over cost on a C+ website can be many multiple times developer turn-over cost on an A+ website, and, depending on the number of different developers that work on your site, developer turn-over cost can be the largest contributor to the difference in your 5 year net present cost when you purchase a C+ website instead of an A+ website.

On the other hand, a C+ website can cost as little as a few thousand dollars going into production (once live), while an A+ website can cost upwards of $30,000.

Moreover, many website owners, for various reasons not excluding budget, choose to use do-it-yourself technologies, which come with high developer turn-over [for the same reason you do not always go to the same doctor].

It makes sense then (and a lot of it), to learn how to build your website in a way that lowers developer turn-over cost.

Selecting a Framework [platform]

You have your project plan in hand, now you want to choose a web development framework.

Learn more about popular frameworks on our blog here:

There are a lot of frameworks to choose from and selecting the framework that's perfect for your situation takes just a little bit of knowledge and expertise, or a fair amount of research.

Once you know a bit more about web technology frameworks available in var_year, there are a few decision inputs that can help you decide on the one for you.

Web Framework Decision Inputs

Do you want a do-it-yourself friendly framework?

Are you adverse to vendor lock-in?

Does your 5-year website have a large footprint?

Do you want to process financial transactions on your site?

Are you anticipating high user interaction (for example: users posting to or commenting on your site)?

Do you want to use modern technologies?

How would you like to administer your website [text editor, browser, desktop software]?

Do you already have a developer or technology in mind?

What security levels do you need from your website, or from your apis that interact with other technologies?

Are you building to an external industry spec?

Can your budget accomodate highly-trained or specialized developers when they are needed?

Do you have a source of highly-trained or specialized developers?

These are just some things to think about when selecting a web-development framework.
