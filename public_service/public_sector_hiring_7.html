Public Sector Hiring 7

Do you need to fill out all of the boxes on your jobs.gc.ca profile?

^

Yes. A lot of information used in screening by PSC comes from those boxes.

Can the computer screen you in?

^

No. The computer can only screen you out. Your job is to pass all the criteria to avoid getting screened out.

What are the four categories of jobs open to the public?

^

  1. Entry level positions [foot in the door]
  2. Highly-specialized positions [can fill internally easily]
  3. Special recruitment programs [minorities, new graduates, disabled, veterans]
  4. Specific targeted jobs to promote renewal [if organization only promotes from within, there’s a risk of narrowing diversity]

What is the monkey story and how does it relate to group think?

^

five monkeys in a caged area can eat any food given to them, but not the bananas, which give them a shock. Replace one monkey, and the others stop it from eating the banana because they learned it will shock them.

^

The new monkey learns, but never gets shocked.

^

Replace all the monkeys over time, and a culture of not eating bananas will exist, even though none of the monkeys in the cage have every been shocked.

What does Recruitment of Policy Leaders achieve?

^

Fast-track up to EC-05 and EC-06 young people who have earned scholarships, have advanced degrees. Connect them with mentors.

^

A way of bringing in fresh ideas and talents

How many jobs might be live (posted) on a given day and how long will the postings last?

^

283 internal and 552 external. Postings last 2-3 weeks at a time, and the categories are always changing.

^

You want a manageable list, but you don’t want to miss out on some potential jobs

What is the first thing to do when you see a new selection process?

^

Print out the list of qualifications (merit criteria).

Where should you address eligibility criteria?

^

90% in your cover letter, 10% with a language test (if applicable)

Where should you address essential experience?

^

95% in your cover letter, 5% in your resume

Where should you address knowledge?

^

85%-90% through a written test, 10-15% through an interview

Where should you address abilities?

^

10% through written test, 80% through interview, and 10% through reference checks

Where should you address suitability?

^

30% through interview, 70% through formal reference checks

What 5 things must you demonstrate and prove to get a job?

^

eligibility, experience, knowledge, abilities, and personal suitability

^

The first two must come from the cover letter and cannot possibly be show in only one page

What should be included in your cover letter template?

^

competition number
position title
classification and level
Personal Record Identifier Number (PRI) (your master ID number for employment with government)

What is a standard format for a cover letter?

^

I am writing you in order to apply for the (title) (xx-xx-level) position in the (section department)(competition xxxx-xxx-xx-xx-xxx).

^

I am sending this letter, and the attached detailed resume, in order to demonstrate my eligibility (A) and qualifications (B-Essentials & C-Assets) agsinst the statement of Merit Criteria.

^

My PRI number is xxx-xxx-xxx.
