Public Sector Hiring 9

What perspective do hiring public sector hiring managers take toward applicants?

^

Hiring managers look for ways to do as little as possible. There is nothing easier than rejecting an applicant.

^

Moreover, they are by legislation supposed to look for ways to disqualify applicants.

^

“It is YOUR job (applicant) to PROVE TO ME that you meet my requirements or I don’t have to interview you.”

As an applicant, what should you definitly not do in your cover letter?

^

Don’t be self-deprecating. Do not give HR any excuse to screen you out.

^

Instead of saying, ’While I do not have XXX experience’ say: ’I have experience that can be applied against the Merit Experience Criteria XXX’

What are asset experiences?

^

Asset experiences from the perspective of hiring managers are non-essential, ’nice to have’ experiences.

What is the purpose of resume in public sector competitions?

^

It validates the information you included in the cover letter by notin gthe jobs you had, where they were, the duratin and times, and what duties you had.

^

Think of it as a supporting reference document.

Give an example of public sector resume structure

^

Job title1, Organization, Times, Experience/duties
Job Title 2, Organization, Times, Experience/duties
Job Title 3, Oganization, Times, Experience/duties

What private sector advice can apply to public sector resumes?

^

Sprinkle key words liberally into your cover letter and resume, using the same words as are in the job description

^

Example could be: swapping out the words ’co-ordination and liason’ for the words ’collaboration with’ because ’collaboration with’ was in the job description

What are the three likely knowledge elements in the poster (job posting)?

^

  1. Knowledge of broad Government of Canada policies and priorities
  2. Knowledge of the Department’s specific mandate or its current policy or program priorities
  3. Knowledge of something specific to the policy area releant to then position

How might a posters three knowledge elements look like in practice?

^

Knowledge:
K1. Knowledge of Government of Canada’s priorities
K2. Knowledge of Canada’s labour market trends and issues
K3. Knowledge of ESDC’s mandate, programs, and priorities
K4. Knowledge of decision-making process in Government for policies and programs

^

Assets:
AK1. Knowledge of process for policy consultations with stakeholders

How to answer the test question: ’demonstrate knowledge of the Department’ (where the job is)?

^

  1. The department’s website [Vision, Mandate]
  2. The publically available ’Departmental Plan’ (used to be called: ’Report on Plans and Priorities’), sent to parliament every year and publically available

^

  • You will likely only need this information to get hired

Where can you find latest departmental priorities or latest governmental overarching priorities?

^

Speech section of Department website [formal speeches delivered recently by Minister]
Minister speeches will usually give a high-level description of all the priorities of the day.

^

Federal budget announcements (every February or March)
Mandate letters from the Prime Minister to each Minister, or the Speech from the Throne by the Governor General each fall.

What do you need to study if you are going for a admin finance position?

^

The Financial Administration Act

What will you need to study if you are applying to work on Memorandum to Cabinet or Treasury Board submissions?

^

Decision making processes of the Privy Council and Treasury Board (respectively)

What will you need to study if you want to be a project manager?

^

Latest approaches from the Centre of Expertise on managing Grants and Contributions (Gs&Cs)

How to ensure good structure on a written test?

^

Memorize short reusable modules, paragraphs, and headings that show you know the priorities, mandate, and processes in play.

^

You should be able to write 15 - 20 little passages that cover all the topics you might be asked without having any sources handy, just from memory.

What generic structure might an AS, EC, or PM use in any given situation?

^

AS - Steps in a problem solving cycle
PM - Steps in a project-management cycle
EC - Steps in a policy-development cycle

^

  1. Start with problem definition/research/identifying issue
  2. Do some research to understand it better
  3. Analyse some options/instruments/policy choices
  4. Choose one
  5. Impement it
  6. Evaluate it and provide feedback

If you have to write a written exam by hand what should you do?

^

Write on every other line. If you have a bunch of modules memorized, spend 5 - 10 minutes just dumping them out on some sheet of paper somewhere (so you don’t have to remember them)
