Public Sector Hiring 7

What is the ’right of appeal’?

^

The right of appeal grants a candidate screened out of a competition an informal discussion.

^

It’s a chance for the an applicant to correct an administrative error, for exmaple if they forgot to submit both pages of their cover letter.

What happens if you put the wrong information in your application?

^

HR will screen you out and the hiring manager will never see your application.

How can the informal help you out?

^

Feedback on why you were screened out.
Maybe you didn’t have enough experience. Maybe the minimum requirement was 5 years and you didn’t have it.

^

Now you will know what how to word it next time or what experience you need to try to get screened in for these jobs in the future.

How are candidates tested for essential (and potentially asset) qualifications?

^

You will be tested on every element of the SOMC
If you said you have knowledge of current trends in health, you will be tested on your knowledge of current trends in health

^

If you fail an element, you will be screened out (and managers will offer informal consultations as to why).

^

In addition to knowlege/ability/perosnal suitability testing, you wlil be assessed by HR and PSC for special eligibility requirements, like language proficiency

Why is the testing phase important?

^

If it is in the SoMC, they will ask you or your reference about it. If it’s not in the SoMC, they can’t ask you about it.

^

If you screw up, the informal will tell you how (you drooled, spoken too fast, not enough content, missed a question, not enough synthesis).

^

If you do not have any given criteria, you will be screened out (so don’t go through the entire process expecting to get job)

What does ’best fit’ mean?

^

It means you can come first on every aspect of the competition and still get screened out because you’re not extroverted enough, or you don’t give great presentations.

What is a condidate trying to specifically prove?

^

That they are the best candidate for a specific-job. The more you can find out about the job and the team, the better placed you are to prove how much you would fit in.

What saying aptly describes public sector hiring?

^

Don’t assume you have the job until you have been doing it for over a week, and maybe not even then!

What is a ’notice of intention?’

^

They selected someone, there is a week while appeals can be launched, then they post notice of appointment.

What does a letter of offer mean?

^

It means you won the competition, but remember, the appeals process is not instantaneous, you can still lose to an appeal.

What notice states you passed all the tests?

^

A notice from HR stating you were found qualified. After receiving such a notice, communication may stop, which means you didn’t get the job.

What should the letter of offer tell you?

^

Your title, which division you were assigned to, your classification, what level you will be (your pay)

How important is the appeal process?

^

The appeal process determines what processes are appropriate for managers going forward.

^

A tribunal decision may overturn an bad process, but it can’t say give the job to ’such and such’, only ’ the process was flawed, do not give the job to ’so and so’

^

Probably just forget appeals as a candidate, another bus comes along

What happens when you start the job?

^

You get assigned a specific set of duties, you develop a performance agreement, you get a training plan, you meet your coworkers

Which departments post to jobs.gc.ca?

^

Core departements must post to jobs.gc.ca, but not all departments. Some post only to their website or to external sites.

If you know of a department you want to work in, seek it out.
