Public Sector Hiring 1

What does a public sector project manager do?

^

Manage the funding of the projects, not the actual projects themselves
Manages funding of companies and NGOs

What are the EC and SI streams?

^

EC are economists and social scientists
SI are statisticians

^

Both groups do policy research, statistical trend analysis, and identification of key drivers in the economic or social spheres of Canada.
They consider possible instruments the government has at its disposal to respond, like:

^

legislation,
regulation,
direct delivery of a program or service
third-party contracting for service delivery
tax benefits
public awareness
direct benefits to individuals, etc.

What is the AS stream?

^

AS is another way of saying ’administrative officer’ stream. This is an overarching category for government people who work only with other government people. It includes PEs (personel, HR) and FIs (finance)

^

Examples:
human resource
accounting
payroll management
scheduling
records management
tracking finances
managing infrastructure (buildings, IT)

Do AS, PM, and EC switch classifications?

^

Rarely

Admin officers (AS) don’t do policy analysis for government programs (EC)
Economic and Social Analysts (EC) almost never switch to AS
Program Managers (PM) can and do switch to both EC and AS, but rarely

Are there specializations within the three broad categories?

^

Yes. Auditors (AU), commerce officers (CO), communications (IS), lawyers (LA/LP), and foreign service officers (FS) also spend most of their time reading, writing and analyzing policy (they are specialized ECs or AS)

^

Equally, the PE category is just a specialized AS category

What is a cover letter?

^

It’s an argument in paper form to a hiring manager trying to convince him/her that you meet the experience requirements

What should a memo to a minister include?

^

Some background on why there is an issue
An outline of the issue
Your evidence and analysis
Your recommendation

What do ECs usually miss in a cover letter?

^

Proof of ’Oral Briefings’

What are ’regs’

^

In public sector hiring, ’regs’ are criteria that must be met to not get ’screened out’

^

Statement of Merit Criteria

What’s a good example of cover letter ’screen in’ percentages?

^

Percentage of applications ’screened in’ to a job competition?

^

With proper cover letter — 96% screened in;
Cover letter done badly — 25% screened in;
No cover letter — 0% screened in.

Do you need a departmental mandate for a public sector test?

^

Yes. Memorize it.

What are five principles for placing well in public sector competitions?

^

  1. Solid Preparation (develop speech modules)
  2. Remember: interview questions for government interviews tend to be standardized
  3. Others have tips on how to improve and you can learn from them
  4. Even if it takes multiple attempts, it can and will come together for you
  5. Government competitions are predictable

What is CIDA?

^

Canadian International Development Agency

Are government competition selections prioritized by what applicants ’can’ do?

^

No. There are rules for every competition, and those rules differ, but the general emphasis is on ’screening out’ applicants, not ’screening in’.

^

The rules dictate what applicants cannot do. The job of the selection committee is to ’deny you’ the job.

What does ELOST stand for?

^

Entry-Level Officer Selection
