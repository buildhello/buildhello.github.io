Public Sector Hiring 5

How does hiring manager process prove merit in practice?

^

Considered a broad range of options
Had a reasonable and logical approach to the decision
Prove the process was sound and thus led to the ’right’ decision

What is the Public Service Modernization Act?

^

PSMA four acts amended in 2003

  1. Public Service Employment Act (employment, staffing, political activies)
  2. Financial Administratin Act (accountability
  3. Canada School of Public Service Act (development and learning)
  4. Public Service Labour Relations Act (bargaining, disputes, and labour relations)

How is merit assessed in the post Public Service Modernization environment?

^

If any candidate meets all essential qualifications, they are ranked equally among all other candidates who meet all essential qualifications. Managers can then select whom they want based on ’best-fit’ criteria which is subjective.

^

Instead of competitions, there are selection processes and eligibility lists have been replaced by ’pools’

What is the average hiring time from job posting to placement?

^

Selection processes (formerly called competitions) take approximately 6 months with a wide variation in range (from three months to two years)

What are the 8 phases of a selection process?

^

  1. Managers identify a ’need’
  2. Managers formally advertise their needs
  3. Applicants apply and are screened out/in
  4. Candidates are tested for essential (and potentially asset) qualifications
  5. Managers select best fit candidate
  6. Managers formally state intention to hire specific applicant(s)
  7. Managers address appeals
  8. Managers hire successful candidate

What happens when a manager posts a new position?

^

Full job description and list of duties to get classified by HR into Project Manager, Information Officer (IS), or Policy Analyst (EC), and level of work (01,02,03,04)

^

Classification can take up to two years, because of shortage of experts. Managers often repurpose jobs so that classification doesn’t match job.

What are generic job descriptions?

^

Generic job descriptions speed up classification.
Policy Analyst Level 4 EC-04 with no specification of duties can recieve instant classification

Should candidates ask for a copy of the job description?

^

Yes. Manager had to develop that to create the job listing. Also ask for a list of duties.

Then you can tailor your answers to ware the real job, not the generic job description.

Is it better to wait for something in the stream you want rather than getting into the wrong stream?

^

Yes. Unlike in the private sector, it can be difficult and time consuming to switch streams in the public sector

Is a Statement of Merit Criteria (SOMC - SAHM-SEE) the same as the job description?

^

No. SOMC list what you will be tested on.

Job description is what the Manager wrote to get the position classified.

Why do HR review job descriptions and Statements of Merit?

^

To ensure the manager has valid, testable criteria that make sense for the job. And to serve as a gatekeeper to the Public Service Commission website

Who posts the Job?

^

Most departments do not post the job themselves, nor do they handle advertising. \\ Instead, they send it to the Public Service Commission to administer the advertising process.

What are priority candidates?

^

Priority candidates are people who were laid off earlier or who relocated because their spouse moved.

These former employees get a shot at positions before they get posted.

What are the two contact points per competition?

^

One is HR, who approved the job description, and merit criteria, and general enquiries who work for PSC (who control the website)

What do you need to prove to pass the application screening process?

^

You need to prove in your cover letter, and with your resume as a backup, that you meet each of the experience and education requirements.

^

It’s not sufficient that you say you meet them. You have to show how you meet them.
